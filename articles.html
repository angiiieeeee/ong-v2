Trump’s First-Day Executive Orders Spark Human Rights Concerns

On his first day in office (January 20, 2025), U.S. President Donald Trump issued executive orders that, according to Human Rights Watch, threaten human rights. The measures include restrictions on refugees and asylum seekers, expedited deportations, the removal of protections for transgender individuals, tougher immigration processes, and the U.S. withdrawal from the Paris Climate Agreements. Foreign development aid was also paused, impacting human rights defenders. HRW warns that these policies erode freedoms and pledged to continue demanding accountability.º

Sudan: Fighters Rape Women and Girls and Subject Them to Sexual Slavery

RSF fighters and allied militias have raped dozens of women and girls in South Kordofan, Sudan, since September 2023, including cases of sexual slavery. Human Rights Watch documented multiple testimonies from survivors and witnesses describing gang rapes, murders, and abductions. Some women were held in slavery-like conditions for months. These crimes, which violate international humanitarian law, could constitute crimes against humanity. HRW urges the UN and the African Union to take immediate action to protect civilians and ensure justice for the victims.

Meeting the realities faced by racialised LGBTI asylum seekers

Break Isolation Group, part of International Women* Space, is a refugee-led initiative in Germany addressing the systemic isolation and oppression faced by asylum seekers, particularly queer individuals. Asylum policies force refugees into restrictive "lagers," exacerbating social exclusion and exposing queer people to further risks. The group provides peer-to-peer support, community-building through activities like shared meals and retreats, and advocacy against oppressive measures such as restricted financial aid and limited mobility. Their work pushes for systemic change, including the abolition of "lagers," recognition of climate refugees, and policies prioritizing dignity and autonomy for all asylum seekers.

Gaza Strip in maps: How 15 months of war have drastically changed life in the territory

Fifteen months of war have devastated Gaza. The conflict began after Hamas’s October 7, 2023, attack on Israel, which killed 1,200 people. Israel’s response has left over 46,600 Palestinians dead, vast destruction, and nearly all of Gaza’s 2.2 million people displaced. Infrastructure has collapsed, poverty is nearly universal, and food insecurity is widespread. The UN warns rebuilding could take decades, with water, sanitation, and healthcare systems in ruins. Despite a ceasefire, recovery remains uncertain.
